# Branding/ Bulk edits Planning Kick-off

## Attendees
- [[Stephanie Suarez]], [[Chimmy Kalu]], [[Elliott Smith]], [[Francisco Rodrigues]], [[Inês Coelho]], [[James Maggs]], [[Sam Firminger]], [[Megan Bryant]]

## Key Decisions
- Focus on expanding the branding service as the highest-impact solution, with mandatory AI extraction (Pomeli-style) to avoid burdening hoteliers with manual setup
- Any solution must include an AI layer; solutions requiring extensive manual configuration are likely to be poorly received
- Three work streams agreed: (1) extending branding service to persist brand values across templates, (2) bulk edits for repetitive elements (URLs, images, languages), (3) AI layer across both
- "Save your own template" is a quick win to complement main solutions but not the sole solution
- Complete spikes to determine technical feasibility before committing to implementation

## Discussion
The meeting addressed scaling pain in M2: users spend 22 minutes per campaign and 190+ clicks on average, and 100+ users still publish on M1 because M2 is too complex. 50% of Messages MRR comes from clients managing 20+ live messages.

Five solutions were evaluated: expanding branding service, bulk edits, save your own template, styles tab in creative builder, and an AI/agentic solution. [[Chimmy Kalu]] raised concerns about hoteliers not wanting more configuration burden. The team converged on expanding the branding service with AI-extracted brand tokens (colours, typography, assets) from hotel website URLs — similar to Google Pomeli — with manual override capability.

[[Sam Firminger]] initially proposed "save your own template" as highest impact/lowest effort, but [[Chimmy Kalu]] argued it doesn't solve the real problem. [[Inês Coelho]] proposed multiple branding configurations users can swap between. Key data: users who duplicate campaigns have 84% publish rate vs 44% overall; competitors (THN, Klaviyo, Canva) already offer branding themes and bulk edits as standard.

The branding service is old and hasn't been touched in a long time — modifications will take significant time. Collaboration between Dragonfly and Kraken squads is needed for template library work. Elliott tested AI extraction with Claude and it pulled colours and typography fairly well.
