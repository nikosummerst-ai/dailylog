## Discussion
Session covered several emerging AI tools relevant to the marketing workflow.

**Claude Design** was the main focus — a tool similar to Google Stitch that creates UI frames, wireframes, and sophisticated HTML animations. Its standout capability is syncing animations to transcript timestamps, making it highly useful for training videos. It accurately replicates design elements from screenshots without needing source files, and output can be transferred directly to Replit. [[Niko Summers]] has used it to build consistent dashboard tabs. [[Valentina Goldie]] and [[Raquel Losilla]] will test it on last year's DBS video for post-event content.

**ChatGPT ImageGen 2.0** was discussed — features deep-thinking image generation, produces up to 8 self-validated variations, and leads the field for multilingual text in images. Some limitations with image-to-image based on [[Guest Personas]] workflow research.

[[Niko Summers]] presented **Obsidian** as a knowledge management system — a Wiki-style local workspace loaded with all Triptease tools and Notion documentation, providing enhanced context for LLMs without manual input. The team is considering a session to set everyone up.

[[Tobias Gunkel]] is currently using Seed Dance for video automation and exploring ways to turn landing pages into videos.

[[Curtis Hurd-McFarlane]] is in their first week and settling in well.
